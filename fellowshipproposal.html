Fellowship Proposal

Topic: Comprehensive Antenatal Care Clinical Models

  • Background

    • Antenatal care is a phase in the life course that spans many aspects of community and clinical management, as such it is often plagued with fragmented and non-standardized data capture. In low and middle income countries (LMICs) ANC services are further hindered by a mostly paper-based record management system.

    The WHO cites poor quality of care and poor accountability of health systems as two of the factors that prevent women from seeking care during this critical phase of life. These are factors that could be improved by creating systems that can maintain continuity of care while providing high quality data for greater decision-making capabilities. Beyond providing guidelines for what information systems should look like, many countries, like LMICs, require a head start in implementing vendor neutral EHRs that will give them greater flexibility and reduce the possibility of vendor lock-in that can increase the cost of maintaining their EHRs and healthcare programs.

  • Aims and Objectives

    • To improve the quality of maternal and child health data through the development and validation of openEHR archetypes and templates.

    The project will focus on creating archetypes using the World Health Organisation (WHO) Digital Adaptation Kit (DAK) for Antenatal Care (ANC). The DAK cover the key areas of maternal heath which include, antenatal screening, social and physical assessments. DAKs are the operational requirements for implementing WHO recommendations in digital systems. The DAK also provides a high level of interoperability ensuring that data points are mapped to other standards-based terminology such as SNOMED-CT.

    1. To model and validate archetypes for antenatal care.

    2. To create a template for the standardized DAK.

    3. To create templates based on local antenatal care guidelines and stakeholder feedback.

    4. To develop a mapping between the WHO DAK and the openEHR archetypes.

  • Modelling Methodology

    • The methodology for this project will be:

    1. Literature review of the WHO and local MCH guidelines and standards.

    2. Archetype development and validation using the CKM and Archetype Designer.

    3. Template creation using Better Archetype Designer.

    4. Develop a comprehensive map of the WHO DAK and the openEHR archetypes.

    5. Peer review and validation using iterative feedback with MCH professionals.

  • Relevance to openEHR

    • This project supports the objectives of the openEHR community. The project may also form a bridge for greater collaboration with the WHO in their goal of providing countries with reusable digital health components.
